I believe Andrew Carnegie should be remembered as an industrious captain of industry rather than a robber baron. I have three specific reasons for this.

First of all, he significantly drove national economic growth. By revolutionizing the steel industry, he provided the essential materials needed to build modern American cities and railroads.

Moreover, his businesses created thousands of jobs, offering livelihoods to countless immigrants and citizens during a critical period of development.

Lastly, his incredible philanthropy justifies this title. He donated his fortune to build thousands of public libraries. As a student who frequently uses local libraries to study and read, I directly benefit from the educational opportunities he helped create.

For these reasons, despite the harsh business practices of his time, Carnegie’s ultimate contributions to the economy, employment, and education make him a true captain of industry.

[ Question 4 ] 노숙인 지원에 대한 사회의 책임 여부

I strongly agree that society is responsible for helping homeless people. I have three logical reasons for this.

First of all, homelessness is primarily a systemic economic issue rather than just a personal failure. Severe inflation and a lack of affordable housing push many ordinary workers onto the streets after sudden job losses or medical emergencies.

Moreover, leaving the homeless without support creates greater financial burdens for the community. Studies show that providing basic housing and preventive healthcare is actually cheaper for the government than paying for their frequent emergency room visits and law enforcement costs.

Lastly, effective social support helps them become contributing members of society again. When I researched social welfare programs for a school project, I found that providing job training for the homeless significantly increases their employment rate, turning them into active taxpayers.

For these reasons, society must take responsibility for helping the homeless, not just out of sympathy, but as a practical investment for a stable economy.
